Here are today's top 5 latest AI and tech news stories.

### 1. SpaceX's Mega IPO Combined with AI Infrastructure
Elon Musk's SpaceX is drawing market attention by pursuing an initial public offering (IPO) worth up to $86 billion. Beyond the simple space industry, this IPO encompasses a vision that combines space internet with AI infrastructure and data centers. It is evaluated as a **crucial barometer testing market confidence in the AI investment boom**.
[Source URL](https://www.bloomberg.com/technology)

### 2. "Power is the Core of AI Investment" - Global Investors Focus on Infrastructure
Major global investment firms like KKR point to the surge in power demand caused by the expansion of AI data centers, emphasizing that "the clearest revenue model for AI investment lies in power grids and infrastructure." Consequently, analysts suggest that the power sector, including natural gas generation and nuclear energy, has entered a new core growth cycle in the AI era.
[Source URL](https://www.cnbc.com/technology)

### 3. Accelerated "Workforce Efficiency" in US Tech Employment Driven by AI Adoption
Job cuts in the US tech sector for May reached their highest level in 21 months. Challenger, Gray & Christmas, a global employment outplacement firm, explained that rather than simple business deterioration, companies are citing **drastic workforce efficiency and automation due to AI adoption** as the primary reason for the layoffs.
[Source URL](https://techcrunch.com/category/artificial-intelligence)

### 4. Meta Delays Release of 'Muse Spark' Developer Tools
The release of developer API tools for Meta's new generative AI model, "Muse Spark," is being delayed longer than expected. This raises concerns about potential short-term setbacks in Meta's strategy to expand its open-source AI ecosystem and monetize through B2B channels.
[Source URL](https://www.theverge.com/tech)

### 5. Global Rush to Legislate AI Regulations and Warnings on Cybersecurity Threats
Specific bills regarding chatbot safety and restrictions on AI in healthcare are passing one after another at the local government level, including in states like Illinois and Colorado in the US. Furthermore, at the "AI+ Power 2026" conference in Hong Kong, strong concerns were raised that future AI agents could be exploited as tools for automated large-scale cyberattacks, bringing the need for a robust security framework to the forefront.
[Source URL](https://www.zdnet.com/article/artificial-intelligence)
