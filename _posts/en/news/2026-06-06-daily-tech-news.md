Here are today's top 5 latest AI and tech news stories.

### 1. US Government Considers Acquiring Stakes in AI Companies and Announces National Security Strategy
The US administration has defined AI technology as the core of national security and mandated government-led cybersecurity testing for leading AI models. What is particularly noteworthy is that the government is actively considering securing stakes in AI research and development companies to share profits with the American people and strengthen state control.
[Source URL](https://www.whitehouse.gov)

### 2. Global Stock Markets Take a Breather from the AI Rally... Correction Phase Centered on Tech Stocks
The rally in AI-related stocks that had been heating up global stock markets has taken a brief pause. As investors' standards for profitable growth become stricter, a correction phase centered on semiconductors and tech stocks is emerging, as the market begins to differentiate companies that can generate actual earnings from simple AI-themed stocks.
[Source URL](https://home.saxo)

### 3. A New Risk in AI Infrastructure Expansion: 'Beyond Power to Water Shortages'
While it is well known that AI data centers consume massive amounts of electricity, the 'water shortage' phenomenon caused by server cooling has recently emerged as a new core risk. Managing the enormous amounts of water consumed during the training and inference processes of giant AI models has become a major challenge for big tech companies.
[Source URL](https://imfounder.com)

### 4. Supermicro Unveils Gigawatt-Class Data Center Based on NVIDIA's 'Vera Rubin'
Major server manufacturing companies, including Supermicro, have successively unveiled gigawatt (GW)-class ultra-large data center solutions based on NVIDIA's next-generation AI platform, the "Vera Rubin" architecture. As the competition to build next-generation infrastructure accelerates, the battle for supremacy in the hardware market is expected to intensify further.
[Source URL](https://zdnet.co.kr)

### 5. Canadian Government Announces National AI Strategy, 'AI for All'
The Canadian government has officially announced a national strategy called "AI for All" to foster its domestic AI industry and strengthen the overall competitiveness of its ecosystem. Covering everything from research and development to real-life applications for citizens and ethical guidelines, it expresses a strong policy commitment to maintaining AI leadership within the North American region.
[Source URL](https://www.canada.ca)
